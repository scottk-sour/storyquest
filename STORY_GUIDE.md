## üåü Story Overview

**Title:** Finding My Safe Place
**Target Age:** 4-6 years
**Duration:** 10 minutes
**Category:** Safety & Security

**Therapeutic Themes:**
- Safety and feeling secure
- Trust building
- Emotional regulation
- Resilience

**Trauma Topics Addressed:**
- Separation and loss
- Feeling unsafe
- Building new attachments

---

## üéØ Therapeutic Goals

This story helps children:

1. **Recognize what makes them feel safe**
   - Identifying safe places
   - Identifying safe people
   - Understanding comfort objects

2. **Learn it's okay to ask for help**
   - Normalizing feelings of worry
   - Building trust with caregivers
   - Developing communication skills

3. **Build coping strategies**
   - Taking time when needed
   - Self-soothing techniques
   - Co-regulation with safe adults

4. **Process feelings about new places**
   - Validating feelings of uncertainty
   - Empowering through choice
   - Celebrating small brave steps

---

## üó∫Ô∏è Story Structure

### Interactive Elements

The story has **multiple pathways** based on the child's choices:

**Starting Choice:**
- Explore the house (proactive coping)
- Find quiet corner (withdrawal for safety)

**Endings:**
1. **Peaceful Ending** - Self-soothing with comfort object
2. **Trusting Ending** - Building trust through asking for help
3. **Connected Ending** - Finding safety in relationships
4. **Brave Ending** - Taking brave steps with support

All endings are positive and validating!

## üí¨ Conversation Guide

### Before Reading (5 minutes)

**Questions to Ask:**
- "Have you ever been somewhere new? How did it feel?"
- "What makes you feel safe and cozy?"
- "Who are the people who help you feel safe?"

**Activities:**
- Let the child choose a comfort item to hold
- Ask them to show you their favorite safe spot
- Normalize feelings about new places

**Watch For:**
- Signs of anxiety about the topic
- Comfort objects they naturally choose
- Openness to discussing feelings

---

### During Reading (10 minutes)

**Engagement Points:**

When Luna first feels worried:
- "What do you think Luna is feeling?"
- "What would you do if you were Luna?"

When Alex speaks gently:
- "Is Alex being kind to Luna? How can you tell?"
- "What nice things is Alex doing?"

At choice points:
- "Which one would you choose? Why?"
- Let the child make all the choices

**Watch For:**
- Which choices the child makes consistently
- Identification with Luna's feelings
- Any signs of distress or triggering
- Moments of engagement or connection

**If the child seems distressed:**
- Pause the story
- Ask "Are you okay? Do you need a break?"
- Remind them "This is just a story. You're safe here with me."
- Offer to switch to a different story

---

### After Reading (10-15 minutes)

**Processing Questions:**
- "How do you think Luna feels at the end?"
- "What helped Luna start to feel safer?"
- "Do you have a 'safe person' like Alex in your life?"
- "What are YOUR safe places?"
- "If you felt worried like Luna, what could you do?"

**Activities:**

1. **Draw Your Safe Place**
   - Provide paper and crayons
   - Ask: "Can you draw or tell me about your safe place?"
   - Help them identify specific details

2. **Make a Safe People List**
   - "Who are the people who help you feel safe?"
   - Create a visual reminder they can reference

3. **Create a Calm-Down Plan**
   - "When you feel worried, what helps you feel better?"
   - List strategies: deep breaths, comfort objects, asking for help
   - Practice together

4. **Practice Asking for Help**
   - Role-play: "Can you help me feel safe?"
   - Celebrate their bravery in asking

**Watch For:**
- Can they identify safe people/places?
- Understanding of self-soothing strategies
- Comfort level asking for help
- Insights into their attachment patterns

## üéì Professional Notes

### Clinical Considerations

**This story is appropriate for:**
- Children adjusting to new placements
- Children working on attachment issues
- Children experiencing anxiety
- Children learning emotional regulation
- Children in early stages of trust-building

**Use caution if:**
- Child has severe trauma triggers around homes/caregivers
- Recent traumatic event (wait 2-4 weeks)
- Child is in crisis (address immediate needs first)

### Therapeutic Opportunities

**The story naturally creates opportunities to:**

1. **Assess attachment style**
   - Does child choose connection or withdrawal?
   - How do they respond to Alex's gentle approach?
   - Comfort with proximity to caregivers?

2. **Build trust**
   - Model Alex's gentle, patient approach
   - Validate all of the child's feelings
   - Keep promises (like Alex does)

3. **Teach emotional regulation**
   - Name feelings as they arise
   - Model calming strategies
   - Celebrate coping successes

4. **Normalize experiences**
   - "Lots of children feel this way"
   - "It's okay to take time to feel safe"
   - "You're not alone"

### Integration with Therapy

**Before using this story:**
- Review the child's trauma history
- Check for specific triggers
- Prepare calming strategies
- Have comfort items ready

**After the story:**
- Document the child's responses
- Share insights with the care team
- Plan follow-up activities
- Monitor for delayed reactions

**Repeat use:**
- Children may benefit from hearing this story multiple times
- Each time may reveal new insights
- Different choices may indicate growth
- Use as a check-in tool

## üè† For Foster Carers & Parents

### How to Use This Story

**Create a calm environment:**
- Find a cozy spot
- Minimize distractions
- Have comfort items nearby
- Allow plenty of time (no rushing)

**Be present:**
- Sit close (but respect their space)
- Pay attention to their reactions
- Pause if they want to talk
- Validate all their feelings

**Follow their lead:**
- Let them make all the choices
- Don't push if they're not ready
- It's okay if they want to stop
- Come back to it another day

**After the story:**
- Talk about what Luna learned
- Make connections to their life
- Celebrate their bravery in listening
- Reinforce that you're their "safe person"

### Red Flags to Watch For

**Contact the child's social worker if:**
- The child becomes very distressed
- They express thoughts of self-harm
- They disclose new information about abuse
- They show regressive behaviors after the story
- They refuse to engage with you afterward

**These are normal reactions:**
- Asking to hear it again and again
- Crying during emotional parts
- Wanting to talk about their experiences
- Making different choices each time
- Asking questions about their situation

## üìä Expected Outcomes

### Short-term (After 1-3 readings):
- Child can name 1-2 things that make them feel safe
- Beginning to identify safe people
- More willing to express when they feel worried
- Starting to use comfort strategies

### Medium-term (After 1-2 weeks):
- Refers to their "safe place" in daily life
- More comfortable asking for help
- Shows understanding of Alex-like caregiving
- Uses story language ("I'm taking time like Luna")

### Long-term (After 1-3 months):
- Internalizes safety concepts
- Stronger attachment to caregivers
- Better emotional regulation
- Reduced anxiety in new situations
- Increased sense of security
