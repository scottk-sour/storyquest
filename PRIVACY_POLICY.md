## Introduction

Welcome to StoryQuest ("we," "our," or "us"). We are committed to protecting the privacy and security of children in care and their caregivers.

This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, and protect your information when you use our therapeutic storytelling platform.

**Important:** StoryQuest is designed specifically for vulnerable children. We take data protection extremely seriously and comply with UK GDPR, the Data Protection Act 2018, and the Children's Code (Age Appropriate Design Code).

---

## 1. Information We Collect

### 1.1 Information You Provide to Us

**Account Information:**
- Email address
- Name
- Role (Parent, Professional, Admin)
- Password (encrypted)

**Child Profile Information:**
- Child's first name (no surname required)
- Age
- Age group (4-6, 7-10)
- Care status (foster care, kinship care, etc.)
- Therapeutic profile (trauma history, triggers, goals) - optional

**Professional Verification (for professionals only):**
- Job title
- Organization name
- DBS certificate number
- Professional registration number

**Reading Session Data:**
- Stories read
- Choices made during stories
- Time spent reading
- Completion status
- Emotional responses (optional)

**Therapeutic Notes (professionals only):**
- Observations about child's progress
- Clinical notes
- Safeguarding concerns (if flagged)

### 1.2 Information We Collect Automatically

**Usage Data:**
- Pages visited
- Stories browsed
- Session duration
- Device type
- Browser type
- IP address (anonymized)

**Analytics:**
- We use privacy-focused analytics to understand how the platform is used
- All data is aggregated and anonymized
- No personal data is shared with third-party analytics providers

### 1.3 Information We Do NOT Collect

We **never** collect:
- Child's full legal name
- Child's surname
- Child's exact date of birth
- Child's address
- Child's school
- Child's social worker's name (unless voluntarily provided)
- Precise geolocation
- Biometric data
- Racial or ethnic origin
- Political opinions
- Religious beliefs
- Health data beyond trauma categories

## 5. Data Security

### 5.1 Technical Safeguards

- **Encryption in transit:** All data encrypted using TLS 1.3
- **Encryption at rest:** Database encryption enabled
- **Secure authentication:** Passwords hashed with bcrypt (10 rounds)
- **Session security:** JWT tokens with 7-day expiry
- **Access controls:** Role-based permissions
- **Audit logging:** All access to child data is logged

### 5.2 Organizational Safeguards

- **Staff training:** All team members trained in data protection
- **DBS checks:** All staff working with child data are DBS checked
- **Incident response:** 72-hour breach notification procedure
- **Regular audits:** Quarterly security reviews
- **Third-party vetting:** All vendors undergo security assessment

### 5.3 Data Retention

| Data Type | Retention Period | Justification |
|-----------|------------------|---------------|
| Active user accounts | While account is active | Service provision |
| Child profiles | While account is active + 3 months | Allows for reactivation |
| Reading session data | While account is active + 1 year | Therapeutic continuity |
| Therapeutic notes | 7 years after last note | Clinical best practice |
| Safeguarding logs | 25 years | Legal requirement (KCSIE) |
| Deleted account data | 30 days (then permanently deleted) | Allows for recovery |
| Audit logs | 2 years | Compliance requirement |

## 13. Glossary

**Child in Care:** A child under the care of a Local Authority, including foster care, kinship care, residential care, or those adopted from care.

**DBS Check:** Disclosure and Barring Service check (criminal records check for working with children).

**GDPR:** General Data Protection Regulation (UK version).

**Local Authority:** The council responsible for child protection and social services.

**Therapeutic Story:** An interactive story designed to help children process difficult experiences.

**Professional:** A verified social worker, therapist, foster carer, or other professional working with children in care.
